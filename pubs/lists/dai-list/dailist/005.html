Send other requests, such as changes in your e-mail address, to DAI-List-Request@mcc.com. ---------------------------------------------------------------------- Date: Tue, 27 Mar 90 11:41:43 CST From: Bernardo Huberman Subject: New paper available "DYNAMICS OF RESOURCE ALLOCATION IN DISTRIBUTED SYSTEMS" E. Lumer and B. A. Huberman Xerox PARC Abstract. We study the dynamics of resource allocation in computer networks and show that, when process transfer rates depend on the number of tasks executing in the system, fairness of use cannot be achieved beyond a critical size. We then demonstrate how the establishment of a hierarchy of process transfer rates allows for optimal and stable dynamical resource allocation in very large systems. These results are used to introduce a suitable hierarchical control structure that achieves optimal load balance with little overhead in communication. We also study the effects of information delays on fairness of use and show that oscillations in resource utilization appear when such delays exceed a certain value. These oscillations can be prevented by the establishment of informal links in the organization, in agreement with observations in the social arena.

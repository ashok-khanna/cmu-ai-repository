Ginsberg) Subject: CIRL: graduate research fellowships available in AI Date: Fri Feb 17 19:47:39 EST 1995 Organization: Computer Science Department, University Oregon. The Computational Intelligence Research Laboratory (CIRL) would like to encourage students interested in doctoral studies in formal aspects of AI to consider graduate school at the University of Oregon in Eugene. Research at CIRL primarily centers on knowledge representation and reasoning, with an emphasis on constraint satisfaction and reasoning about action and physical devices. Faculty members include: James M. Crawford David W. Etherington Matthew L. Ginsberg Joseph C. Pemberton We plan to hire one additional faculty member to join us in the fall. CIRL has funds available to support up to four new Ph.D. students next year. The lab fosters an intimate relationship among a small group of researchers working in closely-related areas and the graduate students we supervise. The existing CIRL faculty members currently supervise five graduate students, two of whom will be graduating this year. Eugene is a community of approximately 150,000 located just west of the Cascades in Oregon. An hour and a half from both skiing and the ocean, it offers a wide range of outdoor activities and a reasonable cost of living. If you are interested in more details about CIRL, please feel free to get in touch with any of us or to look at our WWW page at http://cirl.uoregon.edu. Applications should be made through the University of Oregon Computer and Information Sciences department. Details on the CIS program and application materials can be obtained from betty@cs.uoregon.edu. The deadline for applying to the Ph.D. program at the University of Oregon is March 1, although extensions can be granted for exceptionally qualified students. Thanks!

Date: Wed, 17 Apr 1996 09:24:21 -0500 From: Amy Unruh Sender: ai@A.GP.CS.CMU.EDU There are two or three position openings in the InfoSleuth Project at MCC. PROJECT DESCRIPTION: The InfoSleuth Project involves the use of agent and database technology to support information management, discovery, and analysis in a dynamically changing environment that can include Internet-accessible data as well as enterprise-wide heterogenous information sources. Project research areas include: * Heterogeneous databases (querying, resolution of heterogeneity) * The use of cooperating, adaptive, and autonomous agents for information access, and the use of supporting technologies such as KQML, Java, etc. * The generation and use of workflows/nomadic transactions for information management and discovery. REQUIREMENTS: Candidates must have a background in distributed systems, and either a background in multi-database systems; or AI, with an emphasis on DAI, agent architectures, and planning. Candidates must hold a PhD, or MS with a minimum of two years relevant experience. They must have good programming skills (implementation of prototypes will be required); and good communications skills (both oral and written). Unix background required. Experience with C++, Java, and KQML preferred. Please send your resume, including names of references, to Jerry West, gwest@mcc.com, in ASCII or Postscript.

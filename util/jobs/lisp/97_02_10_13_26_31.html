This position is located in Charleston, South Carolina. The Generative Process Planning Environment (GPPE) is a "Cornerstone" of the DoD's Rapid Acquisition of Manufactured Parts (RAMP) Program. The GPPE is a feature-based STEP-driven State-of-the-Art process planning system. This position involves the enhancement of the GPPE to include feature recognition and other advanced process planning capabilities as well as implementing the emerging ISO STandard for the Exchange of Product model data (STEP) Application Protocol (AP224) ("Mechanical Part Definition for Process Planning Using Machining Features"). The successful applicant will be responsible for providing technical participation, leadership and direction for the next generations of the GPPE. Qualifications: The ideal candidate should possess an Engineering degree (preferably Mechanical) or a Computer Science degree (or equivalent) with experience in software design and project leadership. This person should be self motivated with good communication skills. He/She should have at least 3 - 5 years of relevant experience which includes a minimum 2 - 3 years of LISP programming (preferably in an ICAD (or knowledge based) development environment). Process Planning and Computer Aided Process Planning (CAPP) systems, rule-based systems, solid modeling, and feature recognition experience are highly desirable. Some C programming and data base systems experience would be helpful. A working knowledge of STEP would be a Big Plus. Salary will be commensurate with qualifications and experience.

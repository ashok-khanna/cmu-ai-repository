Seismologist/Software Engineer. The Geophysics Systems Operation (GSO) of Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC) has an immediate opening for a seismologist/software engineer to work on problems of national interest in our office in Melbourne, Florida. GSO is seeking a seismologist with a strong interest in Unix software engineering to contribute to the development and integration of a Prototype U.S. National Data Center to support verification of a Comprehensive Nuclear Test Ban Treaty (CTBT). Responsibilities will include the development, testing, and maintenance of seismological and graphics software. Applicants should have a seismology background (or other geophysical science) and expertise in several of the following areas: Unix, C, Fortran, Lisp, Sun Workstations, relational database management systems, distributed processing, interactive graphics and X-windows. Preference will be given to applicants with strong programming and seismological experience. Candidates must have a B.S. or M.S. plus job experience or a Ph.D. in the physical sciences. SAIC is a progressive and diversified high-technology company focusing on innovative technical solutions to problems in the commercial and government sectors. The GSO specializes in combing expertise in computer science with domain knowledge to develop cost-effective solutions to scientific problems. SAIC is employee-owned and offers excellent benefits, competitive salaries and career-growth opportunities. U.S. citizenship or permanent residency are required. Interested candidates should submit a resume to: Dr. Thomas J.

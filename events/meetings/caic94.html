Participants in related fields such as computational studies of human learning, genetic algorithms and neural nets are also welcome. The emphasis of the workshop will be on informal communication and exchange of ideas. A small fee will be charged to cover costs. The key speaker will be Ming Li from the University of Waterloo, and the general theme of the address will be "combining machine learning theory and practice." SUBMISSIONS: Please submit an extended abstract (of not more than 4000 words) and a short (not more than 1000 word) summary of current work by email (text or Postscript) to bruce@cpsc.ucalgary.ca. A paper will be required for accepted contributions, and these will be collected together in a workshop proceedings as a technical report.

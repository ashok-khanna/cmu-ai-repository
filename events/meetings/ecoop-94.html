Pre-conference workshops are planned to be held on July, 4-5. Workshops will provide an opportunity to meet and discuss issues with selected focus. They will also help to disseminate recent research results and bridge gaps between related fields. Workshop participants are chosen on the basis of a position paper submitted to the workshop organizers. If you wish to organize a workshop, here there are some general guidelines: 1) To encorauge real interaction and exchange of ideas, each workshop will be kept small (preferably under 40 participants). 2) The format of the workshop will be determined by the organizer(s) proposing the workshop, but time must planned to allow general discussion. 3) Workshop can vary in lenght, but the optimal duration will be half or a full day. 4) Proposals for workshops should be approximately two pages in length, and should contain: - a description of the workshop, identifying the specific technical issues that will be addressed; - a discussion of the relevance of the workshop for the object-oriented programming community at this time; - a proposed schedule for organizing the workshop, a preliminary agenda, and the indication of the lenght of the workshop (half a day, or one day). Proposals should be submitted no later than February 1, 1994. Organizers will be notified no later than March 1, 1994. Workshop organizers will be responsible for: - producing a "Call for Partecipation" in the workshop and posting it via various electronic lists and/or other means; - reviewing requests to participate in the workshop and selecting participants; - scheduling the workshop activities.
